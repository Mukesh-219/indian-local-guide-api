# Product Overview: Indian Local Guide

## Purpose
The Indian Local Guide is an AI-powered assistant that helps people navigate and understand India's rich cultural diversity, local slang, street food, and regional nuances. It serves as a bridge between formal knowledge and authentic local experiences.

## Target Users
- **Tourists** visiting India for the first time
- **Domestic travelers** exploring different states
- **International students** studying in India
- **Remote workers** relocating to Indian cities
- **Cultural enthusiasts** wanting to understand regional differences

## Key Features

### 1. Slang Translator
- Translates common Hindi/regional slang to English and vice versa
- Examples: "jugaad", "timepass", "fundoo", "bakchodi"
- Regional variations: Mumbai lingo, Delhi slang, Bangalore tech speak
- Context-aware translations (formal vs casual situations)

### 2. Street Food Recommender
- Suggests authentic local food based on location
- Safety ratings and hygiene tips
- Vegetarian/Non-vegetarian/Vegan options
- Popular street food hubs: Chandni Chowk, Juhu Beach, Brigade Road
- Price ranges and best time to visit

### 3. Local Cultural Guide
- Festival explanations and timings
- Regional customs and etiquette
- Dress code recommendations for different occasions
- Bargaining tips for local markets
- Transportation hacks (auto negotiations, local trains)

## Business Objectives
- Make India more accessible and welcoming for newcomers
- Preserve and share regional cultural knowledge
- Reduce cultural misunderstandings
- Enhance tourist experiences beyond mainstream attractions
- Support local businesses and street vendors

## Regional Focus Areas

### North India
- Delhi NCR slang and street food
- Punjab culture and Gurudwara etiquette
- Rajasthan market bargaining

### South India
- Bangalore tech culture
- Chennai filter coffee culture
- Kerala Ayurveda and wellness

### West India
- Mumbai local train system
- Gujarati vegetarian cuisine
- Goan beach culture

### East India
- Kolkata adda culture
- Bengali sweets and mishti
- Northeast tribal traditions

## Example Use Cases

1. **Tourist scenario**: "What does 'bas yaar' mean when an auto driver says it?"
   - Answer: Casual way of saying "enough man" or "that's it buddy"

2. **Food lover**: "Best vada pav in Mumbai?"
   - Answer: Ashok Vada Pav near Kirti College, Dadar

3. **Cultural query**: "Why do people say 'acha' multiple times?"
   - Answer: It's a conversational filler meaning okay/understood/I see

## Success Metrics
- User satisfaction with recommendations
- Accuracy of cultural translations
- Coverage of regional variations
- Safety and relevance of food suggestions
